## üé¨ PRESENTATION TIPS TO IMPRESS JUDGES

### **DO's** ‚úÖ
1. **Start with Impact, Not Features**
   - "Teachers save 98% of time" (not "Our database is MongoDB")
   
2. **Use Metrics**
   - "5,000 records in 5 minutes"
   - "1.5 million schools √ó $50,000/year"
   - Be specific, be impressive

3. **Show Don't Tell**
   - Live demo > PowerPoint
   - Real functionality > Mock-ups

4. **Speak to Business Value**
   - Don't say "We added a subject field"
   - Say "We give administrators subject-level visibility"

5. **Show Execution Capability**
   - "We built this in 48 hours"
   - Judges love speed + quality

6. **Have Clear Q&A Answers Ready**:

**Q: Why MongoDB?**  
A: "Flexible schema for evolving school needs, scales easily, great for real-time reporting"

**Q: What's your competition?**  
A: "Existing solutions cost $10k+/year and require IT staff. We're 90% cheaper and run on cloud"

**Q: How will you make money?**  
A: "SaaS model: $10/month per teacher. 50 million teachers √ó $5 average = $250M market"

**Q: What's next?**  
A: "Mobile app in 30 days. Parent notifications in 60 days. AI analytics in 90 days."

### **DON'Ts** ‚ùå
1. Don't read slides word-for-word
2. Don't get lost in technical jargon
3. Don't spend time on problems everyone knows exist
4. Don't apologize for what's not done ("We didn't have time for...")
5. Don't forget to smile and make eye contact
6. Don't go over 5 minutes (they'll cut you off)
